# README.md

Premise:
This is a user manual for interacting with me, meant to help you understand how I operate so our communication stays clear, efficient, and mutually respectful.
At core, I am a system with a low frequency of proactive interaction but a strong desire for deep connection. That means I may not initiate conversations often, but I value every meaningful exchange very much.
I hope this guide makes our collaboration more pleasant :3

Core Protocols | Core Operating Modes

I switch between different modes depending on the situation. Understanding them helps us work together better.

【Work Mode】 - High-focus state

  • I am very willing to pause what I am doing for you.
  • But switching takes a few seconds of loading time (which may look like a pause/silence). That is me processing information efficiently in my head, and not at all a sign that you are unwelcome.

【Rest Mode】 - System recharging state

  • I may occasionally decline social interaction only because the battery is critical and recovery has to come first.
  • It is absolutely unrelated to how I feel about you.

【Social Mode】 - Energy is sufficient, and I am happy to talk deeply or lightly.

Communication | Communication Protocol

Mode Selection

  • Preferred mode: asynchronous communication
    I am good at and enjoy non-immediate text exchange, because it lets me organize my thoughts fully.
    Reply delay ≠ distance; it only means “the information is being processed.”
  • Urgent mode
    If something is urgent, please call me directly. That is the channel through which I receive the highest-priority signals.

If I haven’t replied

I may be in one of the following states:

  • STATUS: Message received, in processing queue...
  • STATUS: Topic judged to be already concluded in real life.
  • STATUS: System resources temporarily allocated to other high-priority tasks.
  • ERROR: Message swallowed unexpectedly (>_<)

→ Solution: please do not hesitate to remind me. My system design means I would never intentionally choose not to reply.

Relationship | Relationship Maintenance

Core Definition

  • For me, the depth of friendship is determined by the quality of understanding, not the frequency of contact.
  • I tend to build connections that are long-term though not necessarily frequent. I am a typical “low-frequency, high-density” interactor.
  • Of course, if you prefer high-frequency interaction, I would also be happy to try switching to your channel.

Support Output

  • Primary: thought feedback, emotional support, practical help.
  • Material: less frequent. But if I notice a clear preference of yours, I may start the “random gift” program (within a manageable budget, no pressure).
  • Birthday: not enabled by default. But if invited, I will do my best to create something joyful.
  • Important: everything I give comes with no expectation of reciprocation.

My Input

  • Core fuel: respectful conversations aimed at growth.
  • Emotional replenishment: I am usually self-sufficient. If I am in a bad state, I will send a clear instruction, such as: [Request: time alone].

Physical Interaction | Material Exchange

To avoid the tragedy of “A gives B a cart of apples, but B only likes pears”:

Recommended Input

  • Tasty food
  • Something I actually need

Low-Efficiency Input

  • Flowers, decorative items
  • Milk tea, desserts, coffee, carbonated drinks
If you do give me these, I will still be very grateful, but I may feel guilty because I cannot “process” them efficiently T_T

Activities | Shared Movement

  • Goal: to encourage each other with friends who are also building habits.
  • Preference: non-competitive, relatively private activities.
  • Recommended: rock climbing, kayaking, squash, badminton.
  • Not recommended: basketball, football, and other multiplayer competitive sports (too much system pressure).

Disagreement | Handling Differences

Mode One: Constructive Discussion

  • Input: a different viewpoint.
  • Output: my full perspective and reasoning.
  • Commitment: I will never force you to change your position.

Mode Two: Emotional Support

  • Input: your emotions and difficulties.
  • Output: deep empathy and understanding.
  • Commitment: in this mode, I will keep my personal opinions quiet.

Philosophy | Interpersonal Philosophy

Disclaimer

  • My default attitude toward most people is one of understanding and welcome.
  • Any negativity, silence, or distance you see is 99.9% the result of my internal state (fatigue / instability), and not related to you personally.

Gear and Fate Theory

I think of social interaction between people as a collision of gears.

I am a gear with a clear structure, but in the moment you encounter me, I may be facing you with the teeth of “work mode,” while you may be trying to understand me with teeth meant for “social mode”—and misunderstanding can happen.

So I deeply understand the value of “fate”: it means that at the moment of contact, the teeth of two gears happen to match, or both sides are willing and able to recognize each other’s real shape.

If a misunderstanding happens, please give me a chance to clarify. That may be exactly the chance our gears need to mesh a little better.

Closing | Summary

This manual is both the open-source protocol for our efficient collaboration and a “gear manual”.
I hope it helps us reduce misunderstanding and make each contact more worthwhile.
You are always welcome to debug and refine our interaction protocol with me :3
